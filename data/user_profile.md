### Comp is the #1 filter
- UK VP roles at £90-130K base are instant rejects — even large companies like Elsevier (RELX). UK roles need £160K+ base or very clear evidence of meaningful bonus/equity.
- Singapore roles at SGD 18-25K/month (~$160-225K/yr) are too low for CTO level.
- Tokyo roles at ¥13-16M are too low. Location-adjusted comp still needs to be competitive.
- Roles without listed comp that are at small companies are assumed low and rejected.
- **Takeaway**: Do not present roles unless comp is explicitly listed AND meets thresholds, OR the company is large/established enough that comp is likely competitive even if unlisted.

### Company size is the #2 filter (5/10 rejections cited "too small")
- Seed-stage startups ($5M raises), "founding CTO" roles, and companies with small teams (~20 people) are consistently rejected.
- Scale-ups launched in the last 2-3 years are borderline — only present if clear evidence of real scale (revenue, headcount, funding series B+).
- The stated minimum of 200 employees is a hard floor in practice, not a soft guideline.
- **Takeaway**: Do not present roles at companies likely under 200 employees unless they are on the target companies list (OpenAI, Anthropic, Waymo).

### Sales-facing roles are a mismatch (1/10)
- "Field CTO" was rejected as too sales-oriented despite being at a top AI company (Cognition).
- **Takeaway**: Exclude roles with "Field" in the title or pre-sales/customer-facing focus. Only pure engineering leadership roles.

### Link/posting freshness matters
- Stale postings (2024 role surfaced as 2026) and dead links cause immediate rejection.
- **Takeaway**: Verify posting dates are recent. Prioritize jobs posted within the last 2 weeks. Consider adding a link verification step before presenting.

### Pure leadership, not player/coach (round 2, 3/10 rejections)

- Scale AI "Director Enterprise ML & Research" rejected for requiring publications and being academia-adjacent.
- **Takeaway**: Exclude roles that emphasize hands-on technical execution, require publications, or lead teams < 50 people. The user leads leaders, not individual contributors.
